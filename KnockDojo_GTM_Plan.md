# KnockDojo — Go-to-Market Plan

**Companion to:** PRD, Brand doc, Email nurture sequence
**Purpose:** How KnockDojo gets in front of door-to-door reps and managers, converts to paid, and feeds the KnockIQ funnel.
**Owner:** Zach Tyler · **Status:** Draft v1 · **Updated:** 2026-06-16

---

## 1. Strategy in one paragraph

KnockDojo is a **free, shareable lead magnet** that happens to be a great product. The wedge is virality: the score card turns every practice session into an ad, and the entire audience that engages is — by definition — a door-to-door rep or manager (KnockIQ's exact ICP). We optimize first for **reach + email capture**, then for **paid conversion** (credit plans), and use the captured base as the top of the **KnockIQ** funnel.

**North-star sequence:** reach → free session → score card share (viral loop) → email gate → paid plan / Teams → KnockIQ Pro.

## 3. The viral loop (the core growth engine)

1. Rep finishes a free session → gets a **score card** (PRD §6.1) optimized for stories/feed.
2. Card has the score (the hook), KnockIQ/KnockDojo branding, and a **tracked link**.
3. Friends/teammates click → run their own session → share their score. Repeat.
4. **Amplifiers:** weekly **leaderboard challenges** ("highest score on Greg this week"), persona "boss battles," personal-best re-shares, and a manager-vs-team challenge.

**Instrument K-factor:** shares per user × conversion per share. Anything ≥0.4 meaningfully lowers CAC; design the card + hook copy to push it. A/B the share copy (see nurture doc).

---

## 4. Channels (priority order)

**1. Organic short-form video (primary).** The product is inherently watchable — reps reacting to the AI homeowner closing the door on them is content. Post clips of real sessions (funny fails, clutch saves, hard-mode runs) on TikTok/IG/YouTube Shorts. Cheap, high-fit, compounding.

**2. D2D communities.** Facebook groups (door-to-door, solar, pest, roofing sales), Reddit (r/sales, r/solar), Discords. Show up with the free tool + a challenge, not an ad.

**3. Influencer/trainer partnerships.** D2D sales trainers already sell courses to this exact audience. Offer co-branded personas, affiliate credit, or a "[Trainer] Challenge." Their endorsement is high-trust.

**4. Industry events.** D2DCon, Pest/PCT, solar and roofing expos. A booth where reps line up to beat the leaderboard = live demo + lead capture + content.

**5. Paid (later, once the funnel converts).** Retarget free users who didn't convert; lookalikes off the email list. Don't spend here until organic + viral proves the loop.

**6. KnockIQ cross-promotion.** Surface KnockDojo to existing KnockIQ orgs as a team-onboarding tool; surface KnockIQ to KnockDojo's captured managers.

## 5. Launch plan

**Pre-launch (build + seed):**
- Lock name/domains (KnockDojo .com + .ai), brand, landing page.
- Ship Phase 1 MVP (voice loop, 3 verticals, scoring, score card, email gate).
- Seed 20–30 raw session clips; line up 2–3 D2D trainers for launch-day posts.

**Launch week:**
- Influencer posts + your own short-form blitz, all driving to the free tool.
- "Founding challenge": public leaderboard, prize for top score that week.
- Post in D2D communities (with mod permission) framed as a free tool + challenge.

**Post-launch (compounding):**
- 3–5 short-form posts/week from real sessions (content engine below).
- Weekly leaderboard reset = recurring re-engagement + shares.
- Turn on the email nurture sequence; begin paid retargeting once conversion is validated.

---

## 6. Content engine (keeps the loop fed)

- **Session clips:** fails, saves, hard-mode runs, "rate my pitch."
- **Challenges:** weekly persona boss battle; manager-vs-team; trainer challenges.
- **Educational:** short "how to handle '[objection]'" tied to a persona — useful *and* a soft demo.
- **Social proof:** rep before/after scores; "ramped in X days" manager stories.
- Repurpose top clips into landing-page proof and nurture emails.

## 7. Pricing & conversion levers (recap)

- Free (2 sessions, no signup) → email gate after the free allotment → credit plans (Starter/Growth/Scale) → Enterprise (committed credits). Detail in PRD §8.
- Honest urgency: "you're almost out of free sessions" (no rollover) — see nurture Email 4.
- Manager track routes to a Teams demo; large teams to Enterprise.

---

## 8. Metrics & targets

**Top of funnel:** sessions started, unique users, share-card generation rate, **K-factor**, traffic by channel.
**Mid:** session completion, repeat-visit rate, **email-capture rate** (target 25–40%).
**Bottom:** free→paid conversion, Teams demos booked, NRR / plan expansion, **KnockDojo→KnockIQ upsell rate**.
**Guardrails:** voice COGS ≤15% of session price (PRD §8.1); CAC vs. LTV by channel.

Review weekly for the first 60 days (it's a launch), then monthly.

---

## 9. Risks & counters

- **Cost of virality** — a hit could spike free-session COGS. Counter: lean voice for free traffic, email gate + 30-sec floor as throttles (PRD §8).
- **Novelty churn** — fun to try once, then forgotten. Counter: leaderboards, belts/certification, weekly challenges, and the manager use case (which is recurring by nature).
- **"It's just an ad for KnockIQ"** — keep KnockDojo genuinely useful and standalone-valuable; the funnel is a benefit, not the point.
- **Realism bar** — if the AI homeowner feels fake, no loop. This is exactly why Phase 0 gates on realism before any of this spend.
