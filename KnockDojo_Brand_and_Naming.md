## 1. Recommendation: **KnockDojo**

A practice space for door knockers. A "dojo" is where you train and spar safely before the real fight — exactly the product. It's memorable, fun, ownable, and sits in the **"Knock" family with KnockIQ**, which helps the lead-magnet → KnockIQ funnel (kinship without being a clone).

- **Domains available now:** `knockdojo.com` (~$11/yr) **and** `knockdojo.ai` — grab both.
- **Tagline options:** "Train before you knock." · "Where reps get ramp-ready." · "Spar with the door."

## 3. Runner-up

**KnockCoach** (`knockcoach.com`) — if you'd rather lead with "coaching" than "training/sparring." Clearer to a cold visitor, slightly less distinctive. Good as a redirect either way.

## 4. Brand direction (light)

**Positioning:** *The flight simulator for door knockers.* Practice on a realistic AI homeowner, get scored, walk up to real doors warmed up.

**Personality:** confident, a little playful, rep-to-rep. Not corporate. Think "gym for your pitch," not "enterprise training platform."

**Voice:**
- Short, punchy, second-person. "Beat your last score." "Spar with the door."
- Plain-spoken sales-floor language; zero jargon.
- Encouraging but honest (mirrors the in-product coaching tone).

**Visual cues (starter):**
- **Motif:** a door + a subtle dojo/belt or target accent. A door that doubles as a "ring."
- **Color:** keep kinship with KnockIQ's palette but distinct — a bold primary (energetic) + dark "dojo" neutral. (Pull exact hues from KnockIQ's brand so the family reads as related.)
- **Score-forward UI:** the number is the hero (it's the shareable, viral unit). Big, proud, badge-like.
- **Belt/rank system** for difficulty & certification ("white belt → black belt," ladders to "Doorstep Certified" → rename to **"KnockDojo Certified"**).

**Naming knock-on (update across docs):** rename product references from *Doorstep* → *KnockDojo*, and the cert badge to **"KnockDojo Certified."** The four existing planning docs (PRD, nurture sequence, Phase 0 spec, persona library) still say "Doorstep" — easy global find-replace when you confirm the name.
