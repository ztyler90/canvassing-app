**Goal:** Get door-knocking sales organizations to sign up for a **free team pilot** of KnockIQ via LinkedIn + cold email.

## TL;DR — What you're executing

We're running outbound (LinkedIn DMs + cold email) to owners and sales managers at door-to-door sales companies. The offer is a **free team pilot this season — no credit card, 10-minute setup.** Lead with the pain (managers can't see what reps actually do), prove it with a 45-second product video, and drive to the pilot. Start with **pest control**, then clone the winning messaging into **alarms/home security** and **solar**.

---

## 1. Positioning — the wedge

Owners don't care about features. They care about two things:
- **Reps knocking more doors** (productivity/accountability)
- **Managers seeing who's actually working** (visibility)

**Core promise:** *"See every door your team knocks in real time, and turn that into more booked deals."*

Lead with the pain, not the tool:
- Managers are flying blind — door counts are self-reported and inflated (~40%).
- Good territory goes un-worked and nobody notices.
- No live view of where reps are or how they're performing.

**Our unfair advantage:** the product is *visual*. The live map + climbing leaderboard create an instant "wow." That's why **video carries conversion** (see Section 6).

## 2. Target list — who and how to find them

### Verticals (in priority order)
1. **Pest control** — huge summer door-knocking programs, recurring revenue, big rep counts. (Start here.)
2. **Alarms / home security** — classic D2D, very rep-heavy, competitive.
3. **Solar** — high ACV, large teams, tech-forward buyers.

### Titles that sign pilots
Owner / Founder, VP of Sales, Director of Sales, Sales Manager, Area/Regional Manager. Also **D2D Director** and **Recruiting Manager** for pest/alarms (they obsess over rep productivity).

### Company filter
Headcount **11–200** — big enough to have the problem, small enough that the owner buys without procurement.

### Where they cluster
- **LinkedIn** — search title + keywords: "door to door," "canvassing," "summer sales," "pest control sales," "solar sales."
- **Summer-sales ecosystem** — alumni of Aptive, Vivint, Aterra, Moxie, Edge, Alder, etc. Many have started their own shops.
- **Apollo / ZoomInfo / Clay** — pull verified emails by industry code + title.

> **Rule:** A focused list of **300–500 well-targeted owners** beats 10,000 random contacts. Personalize at the *segment* level (pest vs. alarms vs. solar).

## 3. LinkedIn motion

Run two motions in parallel, from a **personal profile** (not a company page).

### A) Outbound DMs (1:1)

**Connection request** — no note, or a tiny note (note-less often connects better):
> Hey [First], building tools for D2D sales teams — would love to connect.

**Message 1** (1–2 days after they accept):
> [First] — saw you run the canvassing team at [Company]. Quick q, not a pitch: how are you tracking which doors your reps actually knock right now? Most pest/alarm owners I talk to are stuck guessing from pins on a paper map or rep self-reports.

**Message 2** (after they reply, or 3 days later if no reply):
> Built KnockIQ for exactly that — live map of every rep, real-time door + knock counts, auto leaderboard, and it turns knocks into booked appointments and estimates. Easier to show than tell — here's a 45-sec look: [video link]. Want me to set your team up with a free pilot this season? No card, takes 10 min.

**Pattern that works:** question first → value second → low-friction CTA third. **Never pitch in the connection note.**

### B) Content / inbound (1:many)

Post **3x/week** from the personal profile. Highest-converting formats:
- Screen recordings of the live map ("This is what 12 reps knocking 400 doors looks like in real time").
- Leaderboard / before-and-after door-count stats.
- Strong opinions ("Rep self-reported door counts are inflated ~40%. Here's how to fix it.").

Content warms up everyone the DMs touch (they check your profile before replying). It's conversion *support*, not a separate project.

## 4. Cold email motion

Keep emails **under 90 words**, one idea, one CTA, plain text, **no images in the first touch** (hurts deliverability).

**Email 1 — the problem**
> **Subject:** knocking doors at [Company]
>
> Hi [First] — do you actually know how many doors your reps knocked yesterday, or is it self-reported?
>
> Most [pest/alarm/solar] owners I talk to can't see it live, so accountability slips and good territory goes un-worked.
>
> KnockIQ gives you a live map of every rep, real door + knock counts, and an auto leaderboard — then turns those knocks into booked appointments and estimates.
>
> Open to a free pilot for your team this season? Happy to set it up in 10 minutes.

**Email 2 (day 3) — proof / video**
> **Subject:** re: knocking doors at [Company]
>
> Quick follow-up — easier to show than describe. 45-second look at the live map + leaderboard: [video link].
>
> Teams using it stop guessing about rep activity and book more off the same number of doors. Want me to spin up a free pilot for [Company]?

**Email 3 (day 7) — breakup**
> **Subject:** should I close this out?
>
> [First], haven't heard back — totally fine if tracking door activity isn't a priority right now. If it is, I'll get your team a free pilot today, no card. Just say the word.

**Deliverability rules:**
- Send across **2–4 inboxes on separate sending domains** (e.g., try-knockiq.com, getknockiq-mail.com).
- Each inbox warmed up; **20–30 sends/inbox/day**.
- **Never blast from the primary domain.**

## 5. The call-to-action (free team pilot)

Make it the path of least resistance and use it consistently everywhere:
- Phrase it as **"free pilot for your team this season — no card, set up in 10 minutes."** Season framing creates urgency.
- Offer to do setup *for* them on a 10-min call (that call *is* the demo, but feels like onboarding).
- Give two doors: a booking link **and** "just reply YES and I'll send access."
- Optional scarcity: "I'm onboarding 10 teams before the summer push — want a spot?"

---

## 6. Video — the biggest conversion lever

The product is visual, so the "wow" is instant. Three tiers:

1. **Evergreen 45-sec product clip** — screen recording of the live map populating with reps + leaderboard climbing. Goes in every LinkedIn message, email 2, and the profile. Captioned (most watch muted), bold thumbnail.
2. **Personalized video at scale** — Vidyard / Loom / **Sendspark** show a personalized intro frame + custom thumbnail ("Hey [Company] team —"). Can 2–3x reply rates. Sendspark + Clay can auto-generate from the list.
3. **5-min founder demo (Loom)** for warm replies — tailored to *their* vertical. Scales demos asynchronously.

**Hook discipline:** first 3 seconds must show the **live map moving**, not a face. Lead with product magic.

## 7. Automation & scale stack

Most of this is already connected to Zach's account.

| Layer | Tool | Job |
|---|---|---|
| List building | Apollo / ZoomInfo | Pull leads by title + industry + headcount |
| Personalization brain | Clay | Enrich, dedupe, auto-write per-segment opener; trigger Sendspark videos |
| Email sending | Smartlead / Instantly (or Apollo / Outreach) | Inbox rotation + warmup + deliverability |
| LinkedIn automation | HeyReach / Expandi | Connection + message sequences (keep volume human) |
| Personalized video | Sendspark | Auto-generated personalized clips at scale |
| CRM | HubSpot | Track pilot → activation → paid; tag by vertical + channel |
| Product analytics | PostHog | Instrument pilots; trigger activation-based check-ins |

**Volume guardrails (avoid restrictions):**
- LinkedIn: **15–25 connects/day, 30–40 messages/day** per account. Keep it human.
- Email: ~50/day across rotated inboxes.

**Realistic solo/intern-run scale:** ~350 quality touches/week → 5–15 pilot conversations/week once messaging is dialed.

**Key metric:** activation, not signup. Track **time-to-first-knock, reps activated, doors logged in week 1.** Auto-trigger a check-in when a pilot org logs 0 doors ("want a 10-min setup call?").
