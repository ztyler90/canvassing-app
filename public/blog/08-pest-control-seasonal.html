🐛 Pest Control Playbook

Door-to-Door Pest Control Sales: Turning a Summer Blitz Into Year-Round Accounts

The playbook for building a pest control book of business that lasts well past the summer rush.

Key Insight
Quality Over Count
1,400 accounts that stick for 3 years  >  2,000 accounts that cancel by month 4
Sales Quality
The Right Qualifying Conversation
🗺️

Clear Walkthrough

Walk the homeowner through exactly what the service covers — no ambiguity.

💲

Honest Pricing

Answer pricing questions directly, including what happens after any intro period.

📋

Agreement Explanation

Explain the agreement clearly — term, cancellation policy, and what they're signing.

✅

Informed Decision Check

Confirm the homeowner understands the commitment before signing.

⏳

Time for Questions

Leave space for questions. Rushing kills retention and invites chargebacks.

Data Capture
Track Every Door
🔥

Doors That Almost Signed

These are follow-up gold. Track the interest level, objection, and best time to return. A warm lead this summer is your first close next summer.

📊

Doors That Said No

Intel for next summer. Know which streets are saturated, which objections repeat, and where the competition already closed.

Retention
The Follow-Up Engine
📞

Post-Treatment Check-In

Touch base after the first service. Set expectations and catch issues early.

💳

Billing Change Comms

Communicate any billing changes proactively — surprises cause cancellations.

🔄

Pre-Renewal Conversation

Reach out before the renewal date. Remind them of value, not just the charge.

🏆

Win-Back for Cancellations

Have a defined outreach for cancelled accounts. Some just need a better offer or timing.

Operations
Blitz Coverage Discipline
🗂️

Clean Daily Territories

Every rep knows their assigned area before the day starts. No overlap, no confusion.

👁️

Real-Time Visibility

Managers can see activity as it happens — not 12 hours later on a spreadsheet.

📍

Tracked in the Moment

Doors logged as they're knocked. No end-of-day memory exercises.

🗺️

15-Min Morning Map Review

Quick daily standup with the map. Where did we hit? Where do we go? What's warm?

Off-Season
Off-Season Work
  • ✓ Review summer data — what areas performed, what didn't, and why
  • ✓ Plan next summer's coverage using this year's door-level intel
  • ✓ Refine training based on the objections and patterns you actually saw
  • ✓ Tighten CX — fix the handoff gaps between sales and service
  • ✓ Run a light canvass in shoulder months to keep reps sharp
